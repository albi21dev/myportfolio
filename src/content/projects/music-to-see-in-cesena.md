Liuteria Diego Suzzi is the beating heart of Cesena’s music scene, where live music seems to have declared a little personal Renaissance—renaissance being a polite way of saying “chaos, beauty, and occasional mayhem all rolled into one.” Artists like Gaia Banfi, Aka5ha, Baab, Oora, Luca Longobardi, and a rotating cast of others take the stage, filling the room with sounds that sometimes surprise, sometimes overwhelm, and often make you wonder how such a tiny space can contain so much energy.

## In the Dark, but Gloriously So
Photographers, bless them, face the heroic challenge of low light and cramped corners: a test of patience, skill, and the willingness to accept that some shots will be gloriously blurry, and some just gloriously… dark. Yet, every frame—shadowed, tilted, or perfectly caught—tells the story of a place where music is not just performed but wrangled into existence, where the audience leans in, the performers lean out, and the walls lean precariously along with them. It’s intimate. It’s alive. It’s occasionally frustrating. And, honestly, that’s exactly what makes it worth being there.
